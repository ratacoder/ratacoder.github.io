Mapping, Attacking and Defending the BitTorrent Ecosystem

About - People - Publications - Press

About

The BitTorrent Ecosystem includes millions of BitTorrent peers (employing over 50 different client implementations), hundreds of active trackers, and dozens of independently-operated torrent discovery sites. The Ecosystem is further fueled with distributed trackers using Distributed Hash Table (DHT) and Peer Exchange (PEX) functionality. In addition to the "public" BitTorrent activity, the Ecosystem includes hundreds of private BitTorrent sites, or BitTorrent darknets,requiring special privileges for access. The Ecosystem also includes "interdiction companies," which attempt to curtail the distribution of targeted content.

Despite its importance, both in terms of its footprint in the Internet and the influence it has on emerging P2P applications, the BitTorrent Ecosystem is only partially understood today. We have been seeking a comprehensive and in-depth understanding of the BitTorrent Ecosystem, as well as tools for mapping the Ecosystem in the future. The original proposed research has two inter-related research thrusts. The first is to develop tools and methodologies for comprehensive exploration and mapping of the entire BitTorrent Ecosystem, both public and private. The second is an in-depth understanding of security issues in BitTorrent and in P2P systems in general.

We extended the scope of the original proposed research to investigate security and privacy attacks that exploit BitTorrent and other online services in general, for example BitTorrent and Skype; and BitTorrent, Facebook, and Skype.
